# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

Everyone who participates in the PixelSnitch community — as a contributor, maintainer, issue reporter, or user commenting on issues and pull requests — is expected to help us keep this a welcoming, harassment-free space for people of every background and experience level.

We pledge to engage with each other respectfully, to assume good faith, and to focus feedback on the work rather than on the person.

## Our Standards

Behaviors that help the community thrive include:

- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Taking responsibility for mistakes, apologizing, and learning from them
- Focusing on what is best for the overall community, not just for ourselves

Behaviors that are not welcome here include:

- Personal attacks, insults, or demeaning comments
- Harassment of any kind, public or private
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Any other conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces — the GitHub repository, issues, pull requests, discussions, and any official community channels — and also applies when an individual is representing the project in public spaces.

## Adoption

This Code of Conduct is adopted from the **[Contributor Covenant, version 2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/)**, which is the canonical authority for interpretation of anything not explicitly addressed here. For a full reading of the standards and enforcement guidelines, please refer to that document.

Community Impact Guidelines were inspired by [Mozilla's code of conduct enforcement ladder](https://github.com/mozilla/diversity).

## Enforcement

PixelSnitch is currently maintained by a single person. Reports of Code of Conduct violations can be raised through any of the following channels:

- **Serious behavior that violates GitHub's Terms of Service** (threats, targeted harassment, doxxing, hate speech, etc.) — use GitHub's official abuse report form at <https://support.github.com/contact/report-abuse-or-spam>. GitHub staff will investigate independently of the project maintainer.
- **Project-specific concerns** — open an issue in this repository and tag it with the `conduct` label, or start a post in **[Discussions](https://github.com/Arckollect/pixelsnitch/discussions)**. Please note these channels are public.
- **Matters that should not be public** — until a dedicated private reporting address is established, you may open a [GitHub Security Advisory](https://github.com/Arckollect/pixelsnitch/security/advisories/new) describing the concern. The advisory will be treated confidentially even though it lives in the security-reporting system.

All reports will be reviewed promptly and fairly, and the maintainer will respect the privacy and security of anyone raising a concern in good faith.

Maintainers who do not follow or enforce this Code of Conduct in good faith may face temporary or permanent repercussions as determined by the broader community.
